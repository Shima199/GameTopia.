Top 6 Video Game Companies

SCE (Sony Computer Entertainment) has been around for a while. It is a subsidiary of tech giant Sony, which was built around the highly successful PlayStation console. SCE was founded in Tokyo on November 16, 1993, to handle Sony’s video game development for the PlayStation systems.

It would be deeply irresponsible not to mention Nintendo, as Nintendo is by far the oldest company on this list. Nintendo was formed 130 years ago in 1889 as Nintendo Karuta Co. Its purpose was to create hand-illustrated hanafuda, which are Japanese playing cards with pictures rather than numbers and suits.

Electronic Arts, often abbreviated EA, is also worth mentioning for its age. It’s one of the oldest software companies that has remained a successful business. It was originally founded in 1982, and it is best known for its sports representation in its yearly games like NFL, NBA, and FIFA.

In 1999 BMG games were taken over by Take-Two Interactive, and Rockstar games were born. The development company is most well known for its Grand Theft Auto series.In 2014, Rockstar shipped more than 250 million copies of their games.

Potomac Computer Systems was founded by Tim Sweeney in 1991 while he was a student at the University of Maryland. Potomac turned into Epic MegaGames, an attempt to make this one person studio seem larger.

A twin-headed monster in the gaming industry, Activision and Blizzard each are responsible for extremely popular games. Activision is well known for the Tony Hawk franchise and the ubiquitous Call of Duty first-person shooter franchise.
